Vibe

Listening to music through headphones is perfect for having a private experience while you work, relax, or exercise.

However this method of listening loses some of the emotion and tactile impact of being surrounded by music like at a live event.

Vibe is a new wearable garment that brings this impact back to your music while maintaining the privacy and discretion of headphones.

The hoodie connects to your headphones, and your phone, your laptop, or any other audio source, and uses embedded motors that vibrate in sync with the bassline of your music to create a full body sensation to immerse you in your music.

Getting Started

Vibe comes in 2 parts:



A 100% cotton zip hoodie that is meant to be worn comfortably all day.

And a driver box that plugs into the jacket and connects the motors to you music and headphones.

Connecting the Driver Box



Step 1

When first setting up your Vibe, you may consider laying out the jacket on a clean flat surface.

The front right pocket contains snap buttons that connect to the driver box.



Step 2

Locate the corresponding snaps on the bottom of the driver box. These should be in arranged in an identical pattern to the snaps on the jacket.



Step 3

Align the snaps of the Jacket and the Driver Box and press each button one at a time until you hear a snap. Check each of the five snaps to make sure they are firmly secured.

Connecting your music



Step 4

There are 2 auxillary ports on the driver box.

You can connect your headphones to either port and use the included auxillary cord to connect your music source to the other port. The ports are identical, so you can swap to two connections according to your preference.



Step 5

There is a single button on the top of the driver box for turning the device on and off.

Press this button once to turn in on, and again to turn it off. The button will illuminate blue when Vibe is on.



Step 6

Vibe is designed to work with any audio source (itunes, spotify, youtube, video games, movies, etc.).

On first set up you can test that the motors are working by pressing play on any track once you have connected your music source. The jacket should begin vibrating with the bassline of the music

Testing the motors



Step 7

When bassline of your track starts playing, you should feel the motors in the jacket vibrate.

The motors are arragned symmetrically around the jacket, check to ensure both sides are activating.



Troubleshooting/Recalibrating.

In the event that the motors do not activate you may need to recalibrate the driver box. If that’s the case, please refer to "Troubleshooting" below.

Daily Use



Once you’ve observed the motors are working properly you’re all set to wear and enjoy your Vibe. Here is a handy checklist for daily use.



1. When using the jacket, make sure the driver box is on and all snaps are properly connected to the jacket. If any snaps are not secure the jacket won’t work.

2. Make sure the driver box is off when not in use or when disconnecting from the jacket. Keeping the box on will drain the batteries when not in use.

3. Never attempt to open the box. If the box comes open, stop using Vibe immediately

4. If you have a pacemaker, you should not use Vibe

5. Do not use the device while charging the driver box. Charging should always be done while the device is disconnected and not in use.

6. Do not use the device if it is wet or damaged

7. The device will not work with other batteries, do not plug the black USB cable on the Driver Box into other devices.

8. We do not advise taking Vibe into high-security areas, a group like the TSA may be wary of a wearable prototype

Charging

The driver box is the only part of Vibe that requires recharging. If the blue light does not turn on when you press the power button, you need to recharge the battery. The box can be recharged with any standard micro usb phone charger. We've included a micro usb cable for your convenience.



Step 1

Disconnnect the box from the jacket



Step 2

Plug in your micro usb cable.



Step 3

Your box has a red light to indicate charging. When that light turns off, the pack is fully charged.

Cleaning

The jacket and motors are waterproof, but Vibe is a hand wash, hang dry only device. Before cleaning your device, make sure to disconnect the driver box from the jacket. The driver box is not waterproof and should never get wet.

Troubleshooting

If the jacket does not vibrate to your music, try turning up the music. If the jacket works at higher volumes, try boosting the bass on the audio source for a better experience with Vibe.

But If the Driver Box is attached properly to the jacket, is turned on and plugged in, and your music is playing at a reasonable volume with an audible bassline, but the motor’s on the jacket won’t turn on, you may have to recalibrate the jacket. You need a small flathead screwdriver to do this.



Step 1

First, take off your jacket, uplug your music from the device, turn off the box and remove it from the jacket. Be sure to keep the attached black USB cable plugged into the box.



Step 2

Locate the calibration hole on the box near the button snaps. Insert your screwdriver into the hole and turn the knob clockwise till you feel resistance (do not force).



Step 3

Snap the box back onto the jacket except for the 2 snaps furthest from the charging end of the box. Do not connect your music to the box.



Step 4

Turn on the Driver Box, and turn the scew driver counter-clockwise until you feel resistance. The jacket should begin vibrating on it’s own.



Step 5

Using you screwdriver very slowly turn the calibration knob clockwise until Vibe stops vibrating, your Vibe is now recalibrated.
